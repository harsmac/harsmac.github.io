Adversarial Subspace Analysis

Discovering compact subspaces that encode discriminative features in language models

Project Overview

This project investigates how large language models organize information within their embedding spaces. We discovered that a small number of latent directions capture the majority of discriminative power, enabling efficient adversarial attacks and interpretability analysis.

The methodology involves principal component analysis on hidden representations, probing for susceptibility to perturbations and visualizing the resulting subspaces. Our experiments show that focusing on these subspaces significantly reduces the dimensionality required for model manipulation without sacrificing performance.
